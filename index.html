What will happen in the experiment

Short description

The goal of the study is to investigate trading strategies on financial markets. To this end, you will be asked to participate in a computer laboratory experiment. A computer program will randomly allocate summaries of financial assets such as an asset's expected return and its historical pay-performance. On the basis of this information you must decide on a course of action which you believe to be the most profitable. If you generate more profits in the computer program, you will have a better chance of receiving a higher payment at the end of the experiment. No prior experience in trading on financial markets is required.

Concluding the experiment, you will be asked to fill out a short questionnaire. Please note that both the main experiment and the questionnaire are 100% anonymous. You will be identified by an ID number and any information about you will have your name removed so that you cannot be recognised from it. The project has been ethically approved by the University of Glasgow (application number 400160217).

What's in it for you?

You can earn between 4 and 20£ for a completed experiment, while the average payment is expected to be around 8£. The duration of the whole session will be approximately 1 hour. Participation in this experiment is entirely voluntary. If you decide at any point you during the experiment that you do not wish to continue, you may do so without any further explanation. In any of these cases, you will receive a show-up reward of 2£.
